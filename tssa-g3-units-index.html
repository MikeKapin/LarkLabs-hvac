TSSA G3 Gas Trade Resources Units

Complete coverage of all Gas Trade Resources Units required for TSSA G3 (Gas Technician 3 - Basic) certification in Ontario. All materials aligned with CSA B149.1-25 and CSA B149.2-25 code requirements.

📋 G3 Certification Requirements

  • Units Required: Gas Trade Units 1-9 (all units listed below)
  • Code Knowledge: CSA B149.1-25 fundamentals, CSA B149.2-25 basics
  • Prerequisites: High school diploma, enrollment in TSSA-accredited resource program
  • Exam Format: Multiple choice (50-110 questions depending on unit)
  • Passing Grade: 75% or higher on all unit exams
  • Work Scope: Under supervision on appliances up to 400,000 BTUH
  • Next Step: G2 certification after 2 years (4,000 hours) of documented experience

📚 Recommended G3 Study Path

  1. Start with Unit 1: Safety - Build a strong foundation with safety protocols and procedures. This is critical for all gas work.
  2. Progress to Unit 2: Tools - Learn the tools you'll use daily. Understanding your equipment is essential before studying installations.
  3. Study Unit 4 & 4a: Codes - Understand the CSA B149.1-25 code structure and Ontario regulations. This is the framework for everything else.
  4. Complete Unit 3: Venting - Master venting and combustion air - critical for safe appliance operation and frequent exam topic.
  5. Learn Unit 8: Piping - Gas piping fundamentals. Build on this knowledge as you progress to more complex installations.
  6. Study Unit 9: Appliances - Introduction to gas appliances. Connect piping knowledge to actual equipment installations.
  7. Complete Unit 5: Electricity - Electrical fundamentals for gas systems. Essential for control circuits and troubleshooting.
  8. Finish Unit 6: Technical Drawing - Blueprint reading and technical documentation. Ties everything together.
  9. Review Unit 7: Customer Relations - Professional conduct and communication. Important for career success.
  10. Take Practice Exams - Test your knowledge regularly with chapter reviews and unit exams. Aim for 75%+ consistently.
  11. Review CSA B149.1-25 Throughout - Use Code Compass to reference specific code sections as you study each unit.
  12. Complete TSSA-Accredited Program - This self-study supplements but doesn't replace required formal resource for exam eligibility.
