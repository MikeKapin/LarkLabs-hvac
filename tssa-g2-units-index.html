TSSA G2 Gas Trade Training Units

Advanced Gas Trade Training Units for TSSA G2 (Gas Technician 2 - Intermediate) certification. Comprehensive CSA B149.1-25 and B149.2-25 applications for independent gas technician work in Ontario.

Before pursuing G2 certification, you must have:

  • Valid TSSA G3 (Gas Technician 3) certification
  • Minimum 2 years (4,000 hours) of documented gas fitting experience under supervision
  • Enrollment in TSSA-accredited G2 training program
  • Strong understanding of CSA B149.1-25 fundamentals from G3 training

üìã G2 Certification Requirements

  • Prerequisites: Valid G3 certification + 2 years (4,000 hours) documented experience
  • Units Required: Gas Trade Units 10+ (advanced topics building on G3 foundation)
  • Code Knowledge: Complete CSA B149.1-25 (all sections), CSA B149.2-25 propane handling
  • Training: TSSA-accredited G2 program (typically 8-12 weeks)
  • Exam Format: Multiple choice + practical evaluations
  • Passing Grade: 75% or higher on all exams
  • Work Scope: Independent work on appliances up to 1,200,000 BTUH, complex installations
  • Next Step: G1 certification for unrestricted gas work (additional requirements apply)

Unit 10: Advanced Piping Systems

Complex gas piping design, pressure drop calculations, meter installations, and regulator applications per CSA B149.1-25.

  • Advanced pipe sizing and pressure drop
  • Complex piping system design
  • Gas meters and regulator installation
  • Multi-appliance manifold systems

CSA Code: B149.1-25 Section 4, 5

Unit 13: Controls

Advanced gas appliance controls, safety devices, control circuits, and troubleshooting - critical for independent technician work.

  • Gas valve types and operation
  • Control circuits and wiring diagrams
  • Safety interlocks and limit controls
  • Advanced troubleshooting techniques

CSA Code: B149.1-25 Section 3 (advanced controls)
