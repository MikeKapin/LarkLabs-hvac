Understanding the TSSA G2 Certification

What is the G2 License?

The Gas Technician 2 (G2) license allows you to install, service, and repair gas appliances and systems with inputs up to 400,000 BTU/hr. This certification opens doors to:

  • Residential and light commercial HVAC work
  • Advanced gas appliance installations
  • System design and troubleshooting
  • Higher earning potential and career advancement

Eligibility Requirements

Before taking the G2 exam, you must:

  • Hold a valid G3 license for at least 12 months
  • Complete 2,000 hours of practical experience
  • Pass a pre-examination medical if required
  • Pay the examination fee ($100 as of 2025)

Exam Format and Topics Covered

The TSSA G2 exam consists of 100 multiple-choice questions covering:

  • CSA B149.1 and B149.2 installation codes (40%)
  • Gas appliance design and operation (25%)
  • Venting systems and combustion air (20%)
  • Electrical controls and safety systems (10%)
  • System troubleshooting and maintenance (5%)

Study Strategies That Actually Work

Master the CSA B149.1 Code Structure

The B149.1 code is your most important resource. Here's how to approach it effectively:

Effective Practice Exam Strategy

Practice exams are crucial, but how you use them matters:

  • Start early: Take a baseline exam to identify knowledge gaps
  • Review every answer: Even correct ones - understand why they're right
  • Time yourself: Practice completing 100 questions in 3 hours
  • Focus on weak areas: Spend extra time on topics you struggle with

Key Safety Protocols and Code Sections

Memorize these critical areas that appear frequently on exams:

  • Appliance clearances and ventilation requirements
  • Pressure testing procedures and leak detection
  • Venting system sizing and termination requirements
  • Electrical bonding and grounding standards
  • Emergency shut-off valve locations and accessibility

Common Mistakes and How to Avoid Them

Based on our analysis of thousands of practice exams, these topics cause the most difficulty:

1. Combustion Air Calculations

Many candidates struggle with determining proper combustion air requirements. Practice these calculations repeatedly and understand both confined and unconfined space rules.

2. Venting System Design

Complex venting scenarios involving multiple appliances or unusual configurations often confuse test-takers. Study Table 8.1 thoroughly and practice sizing calculations.

3. Code Cross-References

Questions often reference multiple code sections. Practice navigating between related sections quickly and understand how different requirements interact.

Time Management Tips

  • Don't spend too long on difficult questions - mark them and return later
  • Budget 1.8 minutes per question - this leaves time for review
  • Use elimination strategies - rule out obviously incorrect answers first
  • Trust your preparation - your first instinct is usually correct

After the Exam: What's Next?

Results and Next Steps

TSSA typically provides results within 2-4 weeks via mail. If you pass:

  • Your G2 license will be mailed within 6-8 weeks
  • You can begin working under G2 scope immediately
  • Consider advanced certifications or G1 preparation

If You Need a Retake

Don't be discouraged - many successful technicians needed multiple attempts. If you don't pass:

  • Review your exam results to identify weak areas
  • Focus additional study on problem topics
  • Take more practice exams before rescheduling

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the pass rate for the TSSA G2 exam?
The TSSA G2 exam has a challenging pass rate of approximately 60-70%. Proper preparation with study guides, practice exams, and CSA B149.1 code review significantly improves your chances of success.
How long should I study for the TSSA G2 exam?
Most successful candidates study for 8-12 weeks, dedicating 2-3 hours per day to reviewing CSA B149.1 codes, taking practice exams, and studying system designs. The key is consistent, focused preparation rather than cramming.
What materials can I bring to the TSSA G2 exam?
You can bring approved CSA B149.1 and B149.2 code books (clean copies without notes), a non-programmable calculator, and basic drawing tools. No personal notes, practice exams, or electronic devices are permitted.
Can I use LARK Labs tools during the exam?
While you cannot bring electronic devices or our tools into the exam, using our Code Compass and practice exams during preparation will help you navigate the code books more efficiently during the actual test.
