Gas System Safety Checks

Critical safety procedures for gas appliances and installations

LIFE SAFETY CRITICAL
Gas leaks, carbon monoxide, and improper venting can cause explosions, fires, poisoning, and death. These checks are NOT optional. If you smell gas, evacuate immediately, shut off gas at meter if safe to do so, call emergency services (911), and do not return until cleared by authorities.

Phase 1: Initial Safety Assessment

  • Check for gas odor (mercaptan smell)
    Use your nose - natural gas is odorized to smell like rotten eggs or sulfur
    IF GAS SMELL DETECTED: Evacuate, call 911, shut off gas at meter if safe
  • Test for carbon monoxide presence
    Use portable CO detector - test near appliances, in living spaces, near vents
    Safe: <9ppm | Concern: 9-35ppm | Danger: >35ppm (evacuate)
  • Verify adequate ventilation/combustion air
    Check for blocked vents, closed doors preventing air flow to appliances
    CSA B149.1 Clause 7.2 - Combustion Air Requirements
  • Identify all gas appliances in building
    Furnace, water heater, range, dryer, fireplace, pool heater - inventory all gas equipment
  • Locate main gas shut-off valve
    Typically at meter - verify accessible and functional (1/4 turn to close)

Phase 2: Gas Piping System Inspection

  • Visual inspection of gas piping
    Check for corrosion, damage, improper support, unauthorized modifications, flexible connectors condition
    CSA B149.1 Clause 6.9 - Piping Support & Protection
  • Check pipe sizing adequacy
    Verify pipe diameter appropriate for BTU load and length - undersized = pressure drop
    CSA B149.1 Clause 6.10 - Pipe Sizing Tables
  • Inspect all accessible joints and connections
    Look for rust staining, leak sealant weeping, loose fittings, improper thread compound
  • Verify proper sediment trap (drip leg) installation
    Required at each appliance - minimum 3" nipple extending down from tee
    CSA B149.1 Clause 6.14 - Sediment Traps Required
  • Check gas valve operation at appliances
    Manual shut-off valves should turn freely, handle perpendicular to pipe = closed

Phase 3: Gas Leak Detection

  • Perform soap bubble test on all connections
    Apply leak detection solution or soapy water - bubbles indicate leak
    Test: Meter connections, unions, gas valves, appliance connections, caps
  • Use electronic leak detector on suspicious areas
    Heated diode or infrared detector for detecting small leaks not visible with bubbles
  • Check flexible connector condition
    CSST (yellow), black iron connector, or stainless flex - look for kinks, damage, corrosion
    Replace if: Kinked, corroded, damaged, older uncoated CSST
  • Inspect gas meter for leaks
    Check meter connections, regulator vent, meter body - utility responsibility but check
  • Test appliance gas valve connections
    Where union or flare connects to appliance - common leak point
üö´ IMMEDIATE RED TAG - GAS LEAKS
Any detected gas leak requires immediate action:
  • Shut off gas supply to affected appliance or main if necessary
  • Tag appliance with red warning tag - do not operate
  • Notify customer in writing - explain hazards clearly
  • Report to TSSA if required by regulations
  • Do NOT restore gas service until leak is repaired and retested

Phase 4: Gas Pressure Verification

  • Measure inlet gas pressure (manifold pressure)
    Use manometer or digital gauge at appliance gas valve test port
    Natural Gas: 3.5-5.0" W.C. | Propane: 11" W.C. inlet
  • Check outlet pressure at gas valve
    Measure at manifold with all burners firing
    Natural Gas: 3.5" W.C. | Propane: 10" W.C. typical outlet
  • Verify pressure drop during operation
    Pressure should remain stable - excessive drop indicates undersized piping
    Max pressure drop: 0.5" W.C. from meter to appliance
    CSA B149.1 Clause 6.10.1 - Pressure Drop Limits
  • Test regulator lockup pressure (if applicable)
    With all appliances off, pressure should not exceed specified maximum

Phase 5: Venting System Inspection

  • Verify proper vent material for appliance type
    Category I: B-vent | Cat II/IV: PVC/CPVC/ABS/PP | Must match manufacturer specs
    CSA B149.1 Clause 7.5 - Vent Materials
  • Check vent pipe slope and support
    Natural draft: 1/4" per foot rise | Sidewall: per manufacturer | Proper hangers every 4-6 feet
    CSA B149.1 Clause 7.5.8 - Vent Slope Requirements
  • Inspect vent termination location and clearances
    Check distances from windows, doors, air intakes, property lines, mechanical ventilation
    CSA B149.1 Clause 7.5.9 - Termination Clearances (critical)
  • Verify vent cap/termination installed correctly
    Must be manufacturer-approved cap, not damaged, proper orientation, screens intact
  • Check for vent blockages or restrictions
    Birds nests, ice, debris - inspect with flashlight and mirror if accessible
  • Inspect vent pipe for deterioration or damage
    Rust holes, separated joints, condensate leaking, sagging, crushed pipe
  • Verify proper vent sizing for appliance
    Use CSA B149.1 tables - diameter and height must accommodate BTU input
    CSA B149.1 Clause 7.5.7 - Vent Sizing Tables
  • Test draft pressure (natural draft appliances)
    Should be negative (pulling) - typically -0.01 to -0.05" W.C. over fire
    Positive pressure = backdrafting hazard (RED TAG)
üö´ IMMEDIATE RED TAG - VENTING DEFECTS
The following venting issues require immediate red tag and appliance shutdown:
  • Vent disconnected, separated, or has holes allowing spillage into building
  • Backdrafting observed (spillage switch tripping, products of combustion in space)
  • Vent termination violates clearances (too close to windows, air intakes)
  • Blocked or restricted vent preventing proper flow
  • Improper vent material causing deterioration
  • Inadequate vent size for appliance load

Phase 6: Combustion & CO Testing

  • Perform combustion analysis in flue
    Use combustion analyzer to measure CO, O2, stack temperature, efficiency
    Target: CO <100ppm air-free | O2: 6-9% | CO/CO2 ratio <0.004
  • Test for CO in ambient air near appliance
    Measure CO in room while appliance running - should be 0-9ppm
    0-9ppm: Safe | 9-35ppm: Investigate source | >35ppm: Evacuate
  • Check for flame rollout or abnormal flame
    Observe burner flames - should be blue, stable, no yellow tips, no lifting
  • Test spillage switch operation (if equipped)
    Verify switch trips if vent blocked - some appliances have manual reset button
  • Inspect heat exchanger for cracks/holes
    Visual inspection with flashlight - look for rust, cracks, flame distortion
    Cracked heat exchanger = immediate shutdown and replacement
  • Verify proper air shutter adjustment
    Adjust for clean blue flame - no yellow, no lifting, quiet operation
üí° COMBUSTION ANALYSIS REQUIREMENTS
While not required on every service call, combustion analysis is STRONGLY RECOMMENDED for:
  • New installations and modifications
  • Annual maintenance on commercial equipment
  • Customer complaints of odors or symptoms
  • Any suspected combustion or venting problems
  • After heat exchanger service or repairs

Phase 7: Appliance-Specific Safety Checks

  • Verify all safety controls functioning
    Flame rollout switches, high limit switches, pressure switches - test operation
  • Check flame sensing system operation
    Flame sensor (microamps), thermocouple, thermopile - verify proves flame
    Flame sensor: 2-10¬µA good | <0.5¬µA = clean or replace sensor
  • Test main gas valve for leakage
    With appliance off, check manifold pressure - should be zero (no leak-by)
  • Verify pilot safety system (standing pilot)
    Test that main valve closes when pilot is extinguished
  • Check appliance installation clearances
    Verify clearances to combustibles per nameplate and code
    CSA B149.1 Clause 8.4 - Appliance Clearances
  • Inspect burner assembly and orifices
    Clean burners, check orifice size matches gas type and altitude

Phase 8: Documentation & Customer Communication

  • Document all findings on work order
    Record pressures, CO readings, leak test results, condition of equipment
  • Take photos of safety concerns
    Document red tag conditions, code violations, defects - protect yourself legally
  • Issue red tag if required
    Complete TSSA red tag form, attach to appliance, provide customer copy
    Customer must sign acknowledgment - explain hazards clearly
  • Report to TSSA if mandatory
    Some defects require reporting to Technical Standards & Safety Authority
    TSSA Fuels Safety Program - Reporting Requirements
  • Educate customer on gas safety
    How to recognize gas smell, CO symptoms, when to call for service, emergency procedures
  • Recommend CO alarms if not present
    Required by law in most jurisdictions - one per floor, near bedrooms
    Building Code requires CO alarms in residential with fuel-burning appliances

As a licensed gas technician, you have legal and ethical responsibilities:
  • You MUST red tag dangerous appliances - customer cannot override
  • Document everything - your notes may be used in legal proceedings
  • You can be held liable for injuries if you fail to identify hazards
  • Your license is on the line - never compromise on safety
  • When in doubt, consult with senior tech or TSSA inspector
