Hand Tools

Screwdrivers (Set)Essential
Phillips #1, #2 and Robertson #1, #2 (red/green) - most common fasteners
Magnetic tips preferred
Nut Drivers (Set)Essential
1/4", 5/16", 3/8" hex - for sheet metal screws and electrical connections
6" shaft length recommended
Adjustable WrenchEssential
6" or 8" crescent wrench for gas connections, flare fittings
Pliers SetEssential
Needle nose, slip joint, linesman - wire work and small parts
Wire StrippersEssential
Klein or similar - 10-22 AWG stripping and cutting
Utility KnifeEssential
Retractable blade for insulation, packaging, wire sheathing
Extra blades in pouch
Allen Key SetRecommended
SAE and metric - for set screws on motors, blowers, pulleys
FlashlightRecommended
LED headlamp or handheld - most crawl spaces and attics are dark
Extra batteries or rechargeable
Tape MeasureRecommended
16' or 25' - clearances, ductwork measurements, equipment sizing
Channel Lock PliersOptional
10" adjustable - gas valves, unions, caps
Wire CrimpersOptional
For spade terminals and crimp connections
Small LevelOptional
Torpedo level for condensate line pitch verification

Electrical Testing Equipment

Digital MultimeterEssential
Fluke or quality brand - VAC, VDC, ohms, continuity, capacitance
Must read AC voltage, resistance, and microamps (flame sense)
Non-Contact Voltage TesterEssential
Tick tracer for quick live wire detection - safety first
Clamp MeterEssential
Amp draw measurement without breaking circuit - compressor diagnostics
True RMS preferred for accurate readings
Capacitor TesterRecommended
Dedicated meter or multimeter with capacitance - test run/start caps
Must discharge capacitors before testing
Insulated ScrewdriverRecommended
For safely discharging capacitors across terminals
‚ö†Ô∏è ELECTRICAL SAFETY
Always verify power is OFF before working on electrical components. Test voltage presence before touching wires. Capacitors store lethal voltage even when power is off - ALWAYS discharge before handling.

Safety Equipment

Safety GlassesEssential
ANSI Z87.1 rated - protect eyes from debris, refrigerant, chemicals
Work GlovesEssential
Cut-resistant for sheet metal, insulated for electrical work
Keep multiple pairs - one for dirty work, one for clean
Carbon Monoxide DetectorEssential
Personal or handheld CO meter - test near combustion appliances
Must alarm at >35ppm exposure
Respirator/Dust MaskRecommended
N95 or P100 for attics, crawl spaces, insulation work
Knee PadsRecommended
Protect knees during furnace service, crawl space work
First Aid KitRecommended
Basic supplies: bandages, gauze, antiseptic, burn cream
üõ°Ô∏è SAFETY FIRST
PPE is NOT optional. Every job site has hazards - sharp edges, hot surfaces, electrical, chemical, confined spaces. Your safety equipment protects you from career-ending injuries. Wear it. Use it. Replace it when damaged.
