Gas Trade Unit 5: Basic Electricity

TSSA G3 Certification - Practice Questions & Chapter Reviews

📋 Certification Level: TSSA G3 (Gas Technician 3 - Basic)

Master Gas Trade Unit 5 with free CSA B149.1-25 compliant practice questions for the TSSA G3 certification exam. This unit covers basic electricity principles, electrical circuits, and grounding requirements essential for safe gas system installations.

📖 What This Unit Covers

Gas Trade Unit 5 introduces essential electrical concepts for gas technicians:

  • Chapter 1: DC Circuits - Direct current fundamentals and circuit analysis
  • Chapter 2: AC Circuits - Alternating current principles and applications
  • Chapter 3: Series and Parallel Circuits - Circuit configurations and calculations
  • Chapter 4: Motors - Electric motor types and operation
  • Chapter 5: Transformers - Transformer principles and applications
  • Chapter 6: Basic Electronics - Semiconductor devices and control circuits
  • Chapter 7: Electrical Testing - Multimeter use and electrical troubleshooting

🎯 TSSA G3 Exam Relevance

CSA Code Reference: CSA B149.1-25 Section 3 (Electrical Requirements)

Exam Coverage: Basic electricity appears in approximately 10-12% of G3 exam questions, covering electrical requirements for gas appliances and systems.

💡 Study Tips for Unit 5

  • Understand Ohm's Law and be able to calculate voltage, current, and resistance
  • Know the difference between series and parallel circuit characteristics
  • Familiarize yourself with grounding requirements for gas equipment
  • Practice reading electrical diagrams and circuit schematics
  • Review electrical safety procedures and proper testing techniques
