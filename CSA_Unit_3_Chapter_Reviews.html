Gas Trade Unit 3: Properties of Natural Gas & Propane

TSSA G3 Certification - Practice Questions & Chapter Reviews

📋 Certification Level: TSSA G3 (Gas Technician 3 - Basic)

Master Gas Trade Unit 3 with free CSA B149.1-25 compliant practice questions for the TSSA G3 certification exam. This unit covers the physical and chemical properties of natural gas and propane, fuel characteristics, combustion principles, and safe fuel handling procedures.

📖 What This Unit Covers

Gas Trade Unit 3 focuses on understanding gas fuel properties and safe handling:

  • Chapter 1: Properties of Natural Gas - Composition, characteristics, and behavior of natural gas
  • Chapter 2: Properties of Propane - LPG characteristics, vapor pressure, and storage requirements
  • Chapter 3: Combustion Principles - Combustion process, air requirements, and products of combustion
  • Chapter 4: Safe Fuel Handling - Storage, transportation, and safety procedures for gas fuels

🎯 TSSA G3 Exam Relevance

CSA Code Reference: CSA B149.1-25 (General fuel properties and safety), CSA B149.2 (Propane Storage and Handling)

Exam Coverage: Fuel properties and combustion principles appear in approximately 10-15% of G3 exam questions, essential for understanding safe gas system operation and troubleshooting.

💡 Study Tips for Unit 3

  • Understand the differences between natural gas and propane properties
  • Memorize key characteristics: specific gravity, BTU content, ignition temperature
  • Learn combustion stoichiometry and air-to-fuel ratios
  • Know safety procedures for detecting gas leaks and fuel odorization requirements
  • Study propane storage requirements and pressure-temperature relationships
