Gas Trade Unit 12: Electrical Systems

TSSA G2 Certification - Practice Questions & Chapter Reviews

📋 Certification Level: TSSA G2 (Gas Technician 2 - Intermediate)
⚠️ Prerequisites: G3 Certification + 2 Years (4,000 hours) Experience

Master Gas Trade Unit 12 with free CSA B149.1-25 compliant practice questions for the TSSA G2 certification exam. This unit covers electrical systems fundamentals for gas-fired appliances including power supply, electrical drawings, measuring instruments, circuits and hardware, and millivolt systems.

📖 What This Unit Covers

Gas Trade Unit 12 focuses on electrical fundamentals essential for gas appliance work:

  • Chapter 1: Power Supply - Understanding electrical service, voltage requirements, and power delivery
  • Chapter 2: Interpret Electrical Drawings - Reading wiring diagrams, ladder diagrams, and electrical schematics
  • Chapter 3: Measuring and Test Instruments - Proper use of multimeters, voltage testers, and diagnostic tools
  • Chapter 4: Circuits and Hardware - Circuit types, components, switches, relays, and electrical hardware
  • Chapter 5: Millivolt Systems - Thermocouples, millivolt controls, and pilot safety systems

🎯 TSSA G2 Exam Relevance

CSA Code Reference: CSA B149.1-25 Section 8 (Electrical Requirements)

Exam Coverage: Electrical systems appear in approximately 15-20% of G2 exam questions. Understanding electrical fundamentals is critical for appliance troubleshooting and safety.

💡 Study Tips for Unit 12

  • Understand voltage/current/resistance relationships using Ohm's Law
  • Practice reading wiring diagrams and tracing electrical circuits
  • Master proper multimeter usage for voltage, current, and continuity testing
  • Learn circuit troubleshooting techniques and component testing procedures
  • Study millivolt vs line voltage system differences and safety considerations
